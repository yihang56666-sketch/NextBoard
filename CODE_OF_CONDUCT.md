# Code of Conduct

Hardware Butler is a hardware-development project, so good collaboration is not
only about tone. It is also about safety, evidence, and respecting the limits of
other people's equipment.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and assume good intent while staying precise about risks.
- Ground hardware claims in evidence such as datasheets, schematics, CubeMX
  files, source code, logs, or bench notes.
- Treat unsafe flash, erase, reset, debug, bus-write, and network-scan paths as
  serious issues, not shortcuts.
- Keep discussions focused on reproducible behavior, commands, files, and
  observed output.
- Protect private data, credentials, serial numbers, board photos, and customer
  project details unless the owner explicitly made them public.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Encouraging contributors to bypass confirmation tokens, safety gates, or
  documented hardware preflight checks.
- Publishing secrets, private schematics, proprietary firmware, or undisclosed
  vulnerability details in public issues.
- Misrepresenting guesses as confirmed hardware facts.

## Reporting

For conduct issues, open a GitHub issue if public discussion is appropriate, or
contact the maintainer listed in `SECURITY.md`.

For vulnerabilities or safety bypasses that should not be public yet, do not use
public issues. Follow `SECURITY.md` and use the private security advisory flow.

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments and issues that violate this code
of conduct, and may limit participation for repeated or severe violations.
